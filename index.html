About Me

Hello! I'm a junior at Georgia Tech studying Computer Science, with concentrations in Media, Modeling, and Simulation. I am also working towards a minor in Industrial Design to explore the interaction of media architecture and aethestics with software development. I've been working with the Georgia Tech Research Institute (GTRI) building a front-end platform for a water purification management system, and with GTRI's VIP research program performing geographic data analyses to inform real national policy development. I'm excited to learn more about Software Development, Data Science, and Machine Learning in the coming future, and to use my skills to support societally important businesses and research areas. I'm always eager to find new opportunities to delve into these areas and talk to experts within them. Take a look at my work!

Featured Projects

Crossing Toad

     

I led a mobile app development team to create Crossing Toad, a video game made from Java and Android XML in Android Studio. I coded the game physics, as well the controls and button UI. My main goal was to make a challenging game experience, where enemies home into the player and have collision detection that prevents them from taking the same path as other enemies.

Check it out!

False News Detection Model

     

A machine learning logistic regression model detecting the likelihood that a news article is "fake news" based on keywords, domain name extensions, and tags. It gathers charged words using their frequency within training data articles already marked as real or fake. Early versions of the model were Pandas-based, but this current one is NumPy based.
