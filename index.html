Literally Me

Hello! I'm a junior at Georgia Tech studying Computer Science, with concentrations in Media, Modeling, and Simulation. I am also working towards a minor in Industrial Design to explore the interaction between media architecture and aethestics with software development. I've been working with the Georgia Tech Research Institute (GTRI) building a front-end plaform for a water purification management system, and with GTRI's VIP research program performing geographic data analyses to inform real national policy development. I'm excited to learn more about Software Development, Data Science, and Machine Learning in the coming future, and to use my skills to support societally important businesses and research areas. I'm always eager to find new opportunities to delve into these areas and talk to experts within them.

False News Detection Model

   

A machine learning logistic regressionn model detecting the likelihood that a news article is "fake news" based on keywords, domain name extensions, and tags. It gathers charged words using their frequency within training data articles already marked as real or fake. Early versions of the model were Pandas-based, but this current one is NumPy based.
